Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hybrid inverter and how does it differ from a standard solar inverter?

A hybrid inverter can handle inputs both from solar panels and from a battery storage system (and sometimes the grid), enabling you to store excess solar energy and use it during power outages or off-peak hours. A standard (on-grid) inverter simply converts solar DC to AC for export/use without battery integration.

How many kilowatts do I need for a single-phase vs three-phase hybrid inverter?

For most residential setups with single phase supply, models like 8 kW or 10 kW single-phase hybrid inverter suffice. For larger homes, commercial buildings or systems requiring balanced load across phases, a three-phase hybrid inverter (e.g., 12 kW and above) is ideal. We recommend assessing your load, future expansion, and whether you have three-phase supply before choosing.

What lithium-ion battery capacity should I choose for my solar storage system?

Battery capacity (kWh) depends on your typical energy usage, backup requirement, and whether you want full autonomy or just several hours of backup.

Is Voltrra’s hybrid inverter compatible with net metering and grid-tie systems in Pakistan?

Yes. Our hybrid inverters support both grid-tie and off-grid operation, with appropriate switch gear and protection in place for net-metering systems. You’ll want to check your local DISCO requirements and ensure the inverter’s specifications match your grid conditions (voltage range, three-phase vs single phase).

What warranty and after-sales service does Voltrra offer?

We provide a standard 5-year warranty on the inverter and 5-year warranty on the battery modules. Our global service network ensures local warranty support, spare parts availability and remote monitoring.
